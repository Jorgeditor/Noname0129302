LuckySearch

Your Central Hub for accessing everything from one place. Find tools, effects, presets, and more instantly.

How to use it:

  1. To summon the LuckySearch panel, you must first assign it a shortcut. Open the LuckyTools Settings (Shortcuts tab) and look under the Panels category (the very first section at the top) to set your preferred key combination.
  2. Start typing the name of any effect, preset, or tool you need. The results will populate in real-time with zero lag.
  3. Collapsible Categories: Search results are grouped by category. They may appear closed initially, but you can simply click to expand them. Plus, any category you open or close will save its state for the next time you open the panel.
  4. Use the dynamic buttons below the search bar to re-apply your most recently used effects instantly.
  5. Click the Settings icon to customize the visual appearance, change fonts, or pin your absolute favorite effects for 1-click access.

LuckyShortcuts

Turbo Hotkeys designed to let you assign individual actions inside LuckyTools directly to your keyboard.

How to use it:

  1. Open LuckyTools Settings from Premiere's Window > Extensions menu to view all available commands. Everything is smartly organized into categories (such as Video/Audio Effects, Transitions, Presets, Panels, Sequence, Clip) and dedicated sections for each LuckyTools module.
  2. Click on the input field next to the action you want to accelerate.
  3. Press the exact key or key-combination (e.g., Ctrl + Alt + X) on your keyboard to assign it.
  4. Select a clip on your timeline and press your new hotkey to execute the action with zero latency.

Note: Complex multi-effect macros are not supported to guarantee absolute stability.

LuckyOrganize

Structure Folders instantly to keep your project bins perfectly organized and visually coherent.

How to use it:

  1. In the LuckyOrganize panel, set up your desired folder names, assign specific label colors, and configure text filters.
  2. Open LuckyShortcuts and assign a custom keyboard shortcut to the LuckyOrganize action.
  3. Ensure your media and sequences are present in the Premiere Pro Project Panel.
  4. Press your assigned shortcut to instantly group and color-code your items based on your defined logical patterns.

Note: Exclusively manages items within the Premiere Project panel. It does not move files on your hard drive.

LuckyZoom

Surgical Framing for revolutionizing your framing with dynamic zooms and custom start/end points.

How to use it:

  1. First, assign a shortcut to open LuckyZoom. Go to the LuckyTools Settings (Shortcuts tab) and look under the Panels category to set your key.
  2. Select a clip in your timeline that needs reframing.
  3. Crucial Step: Every time you move the playhead or want to perform a zoom, you must click the Analyze button so the panel reads your current frame.
  4. In the LuckyZoom preview window, hover your mouse and use the scroll wheel to zoom exactly into your desired point.
  5. Choose your zoom style:
    • Static Zooms: Instantly reframes the shot permanently without any animation or movement.
    • Normal Zooms: Creates a standard, smooth animated push-in from your original framing to the new point.
    • Dynamic Zooms: Lets you define custom Start and End points for complex, continuous camera movements across the clip.
  6. Select between the advanced Grow Mode (for world-class easing using native curves) or the standard Transform Mode, and apply!

LuckyCut

Rapid Splicing utilizing dB-based silence detection.

How to use it:

  1. Open LuckyCut and adjust the dB threshold to define what should be considered "silence".
  2. Select the raw audio/video clip on your timeline.
  3. Click Analyze & Cut. The plugin will instantly process the clip and ripple-delete all detected silent gaps, completing hours of rough-cutting in seconds.

LuckyPod

Multicam Switcher perfect for automating podcast and interview edits.

How to use it:

  1. Sync your multicam footage and audio tracks in the timeline as usual.
  2. In LuckyPod, assign the audio signals to their respective camera angles.
  3. Run the process. The tool will intelligently analyze the waveforms to detect who is speaking, and automatically cut and switch to the correct camera for you.

LuckyNotes

Intelligent Timeline to keep your workflow organized with a visual task system.

How to use it:

  1. First, assign a shortcut to open LuckyNotes. Go to the LuckyTools Settings (Shortcuts tab) and look under the Panels category to set your key.
  2. Use the LuckyNotes panel as your digital notepad. Jot down tasks, client feedback, or reminders.
  3. Type specific timecodes (e.g., 01:23:45) directly into your notes.
  4. Click on any timecode in the notes to instantly snap your timeline playhead to that exact moment.
  5. Paste reference images directly into the panel to keep visual guides close at hand.

LuckyCurves

Perfect Dynamics giving you absolute control over animation dynamics.

How to use it:

  1. First, assign a shortcut to open LuckyCurves. Go to the LuckyTools Settings (Shortcuts tab) and look under the Panels category to set your key.
  2. Select the start and end keyframes of the animation you want to smooth out.
  3. In LuckyCurves, select one of the pre-built dynamic profiles (Ease In, Ease Out, etc.).
  4. Add Custom Profiles: You can create and save your own curve profiles! Manually adjust a curve in Premiere, then use LuckyCurves to save it as a new profile so you can reuse that exact dynamic with 1-click in the future.
  5. Click Apply to inject the selected curve data directly into the keyframes.
  6. To remove any easing and return to linear motion, simply hit the Clear button.

LuckyAnchor

Surgical Control for mathematical repositioning of anchor points.

How to use it:

  1. First, assign a shortcut to open LuckyAnchor. Go to the LuckyTools Settings (Shortcuts tab) and look under the Panels category to set your key.
  2. Select any visual clip (video, image, or shape) on your timeline.
  3. Open LuckyAnchor to reveal the interactive alignment grid.
  4. Click on any of the grid's points to mathematically snap the anchor point of the clip to that position (e.g., center, top-left).
  5. The position updates in real-time.
